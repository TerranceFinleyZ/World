Business

Dropbox is a cloud storage and collaboration service that reported a net income of $335.8 million on revenue of $2.16 billion in 2021. This was the first full year of profitability for Dropbox, which had a net loss of $256.3 million in 20202. Dropbox’s net income increased by 64.7% year-over-year in 2021, mainly due to a one-time income tax benefit of $420.2 million from the release of a valuation allowance on its deferred tax assets in the fourth quarter of 2021. Dropbox makes money from its users who upgrade to paid subscription accounts, which accounted for all of its revenue in 2021. Dropbox has over 700 million registered users, of which 17.77 million are paying users as of the end of 2021. The average revenue per paying user was $134.53 in 2021, slightly lower than $134.78 in 2020. According to some sources, Dropbox has a market share of around 18.6% in the file sharing software market worldwide as of November 2023. This means that Dropbox is the second most popular file sharing software after Google Drive, which has a market share of 31.2%1. However, Dropbox’s market share may vary depending on the region, the type of user, and the source of data. Some of the factors that may affect Dropbox’s market share are: The number and quality of its competitors, such as Google Drive, Box, Microsoft OneDrive, WebDrive, etc. The pricing and features of its plans and products, such as Dropbox Business, Dropbox Paper, Dropbox Paper Pro, etc.
